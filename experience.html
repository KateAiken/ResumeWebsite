Experience

Systems Integration Director


When I started university I knew I would join a design team and I jumped at the opportunity to join another water related one, aQuatonomous. I first joined as a general electrical engineer and was assigned to work on the telemetry of the boat. I learned how to use Ardupilot, a software program that works with a pixhawk which is a piece of hardware that interprets commands from the brain of the boat and translates them to the motors using IMU and GPS input. With the importance of my project I was invited to attend the competition with the team. At the competition we made it to semi-finals which was a lot better than expected as it was our first year competing. With the experience of the competition, several of the executives recommended that I apply for a leadership position on the team to further advance my expertise and help mentor others and further advance the buildout of the team. I interviewed for and was awarded the position of Systems Integration Director, a new subteam created from our first year experience and my suggestions for improving the integration of the subsystems. This year has progressive quite well as my team and I have worked on learning more about the software and using data logs from competition runs to check and improve signal strength as well as prep for tuning once the boat hits the water. I am very excited for what is to come at the competition this year especially with the massive strides the team has made with all parts of the boat.

Optimist Race & Sailing Coach


As an Opti Race Coach I had the opportunity to train racers through the spring and summer season and travel across Ontario to compete in local and International regattas. My work this summer included designing and implementing a spring and summer training program which catered to all ranges of experience in sailing and racing. With the younger sailors I focused on introducing them to racing while improving and evolving their sailing skills and taking them to their first regattas to compete with sailors at the same level as them. With my older sailors I worked to elevated skills there resulting in numerous fleet wins and podium placings. I also worked on engaging sailors as a team with fun competition and games on and off the water so they could bond as a team. After camp hours I performed boat and sail maintenance including the set up of 6 brand new boats, gel coating and other repairs.

STEM Mentor


Volunteering has always been one of my interests as it has allowed me a way to share my knowledge and passions, particularly through STEM mentoring. I volunteered to help my former school's all-girl First Lego League (FLL), VEX and Marine Advanced Technology Education (MATE) teams. This gave me the opportunity to mentor students from grade 6 to grade 12 as each team has a different age focus and skill level. FLL is the first extracurricular opportunity for younger students interested in robotics and, as a passionate member when I was in middle school, I jumped at the opportunity to give back and help the younger students. When I started mentoring the FLL team, it was replacing the robots with a newer model so I designed an organization system, labeled all the kits, and put together lessons and a schedule to get the students versed in the new lego and code. As the VEX mentor I assembled the field and helped the team select a robot to build and compete with, while assisting the students to make changes and design adjustments. I supported the team in their first competition and I am proud of how exceedingly well they performed, considering it was the first year in the new format. I became a MATE mentor after graduating high school, where I attended meetings when I was home for university and helped members write code, came up with ideas and taught them about different hardware. I also communicated with team members remotely when they had questions or issues and stayed in communication with the teachers in order to be updated on their progress.

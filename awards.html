Awards

Schulich Leader Nominee


I was honored to be nominated for the Schulich Leader Scholarship in 2024 in recognition of my academic achievements, leadership qualities, and dedication to STEM. I have had the opportunity to support young girls in STEM by mentoring them, helping to boost their confidence and engagement in technical fields. Additionally, I have led a MATE Robotics team, focusing on collaborative problem-solving and preparing for competitions. By balancing my academic responsibilities with mentorship and team leadership, I have aimed to demonstrate perseverance, integrity, and a sincere commitment to making a positive impact through STEM leadership. This nomination reflects not only my own efforts, but also the incredible teams and young learners I’ve had the privilege to support.

C.D. Howe Engineering Scholarship Nominee


The C.D. Howe Scholarship is a scholarship for students studying Engineering in their undergraduate degree. To be considered you need to be nominated by your university and I was one of two nominations from Queens University in 2025. Students are considered for this scholarship who have significant involvement in the STEM world but also a balanced life with other co-curriculars and academic performance. My nomination reflected my sustained engagement in STEM outreach, robotics leadership, and engineering design, as well as my dedication to maintaining excellence across academics, extracurriculars, and mentorship.

Female Trailblazer


At the MATE ROV competition in my grade 12 year I was presented with the Female Trailblazer award. The MATE ROV competition includes specific recognition for women in the field, often presenting a "Female Trailblazer" award to recognize outstanding young women in the Ranger class. The competition itself aims to foster the next generation of engineers and scientists, including women, by focusing on real-world ocean technologies. It was a surprise and an honour to receive this award recognizing not only my technical contributions, but also my commitment to leading by example and supporting other young women in STEM.

2nd Place in Queens Engineering Competition Programming Catagory


My team and I won 2nd place in the programming section of the 2024 Queens Engineering Competition (QEC) in 2024. As first years we signed up to compete for the experience and networking with no expectation of placing among the older, more experienced. We were tasked with designing a website to address a health concern and we selected mental health. We designed a website to help users track their mental health and a machine learning algorithm to read user responses and generate personalized suggestions for the users. Our final prototype was a simple but functional website and basic machine learning code that were designed to be simple and low stress to use. Despite being early in our engineering studies, we delivered a thoughtful, technically sound solution that resonated with judges and demonstrated our ability to combine programming, design thinking, and empathy‑driven problem solving.

AP Information and Computer Sciences Achievment Award


The AP Computer Science awards is given to the student in the highest standing in the course at the end of the year. Computer science was always my favourite course and working on coding projects never felt like school work. In computer science classes in high school I learned both python and java and completed many fun, interesting and difficult projects such as hangman and 2048. These projects strengthened my problem‑solving skills, deepened my understanding of programming fundamentals, and confirmed my passion for software development.

NCWIT Ontario Affiliate High School Award Winner


In Grade 12 I was given an award from the NCWIT for Aspirations in Computing (AiC) High School students in the AiC Community for their computing-related achievements and interests, and encouraged them to pursue their passions. Award recipients are selected based on their aptitude and aspirations in technology and computing, as demonstrated by their computing experience, computing-related activities, leadership experience, persistence, and future plans. It's an honour to be a part of this community and it was an incredible experience to listen to the women in the community to share their stories and meet students with the same passions and interests at the ceremony. Becoming part of this community reinforced my commitment to pursuing computing and supporting other young women in STEM.

Active Citizen Award


The active citizen award is an award given to high school students who completed over 60 volunteer hours in one year. This is to recognize students who went above and beyond in their community. Through high school I volunteered at many places including my sailing club, a dog shelter, a learn how to skate program and my school. I found by diversifying where I volunteered I was able to help a lot more people and gain more experiences. Most of my volunteer hours that went towards this award were spent organizing and redesigning my school's tech lab. This was a massive undertaking for one person and I spent mornings before school and spares working on it: not only cleaning up and redesigning the space but also compiling a list of upgrades that were eventually funded and implemented making it a nicer more usable space for the future students. All of my volunteer experiences were rewarding, but the tech‑lab project was especially meaningful. Knowing that the space will support and inspire girls in STEM for years to come made the work deeply worthwhile.
